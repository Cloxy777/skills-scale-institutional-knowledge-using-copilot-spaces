# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

## Project Management Processes — Overview

OctoAcme's project management processes are centered around clear ownership, collaborative planning, and transparent execution. Projects follow a structured lifecycle beginning with Initiation—defining the problem, stakeholders, and success metrics—followed by detailed Planning, iterative Execution, Release, and a Close & Retrospective phase. Throughout each stage, the team relies on key artifacts such as the Project Charter, prioritized backlogs with acceptance criteria, risk registers, and communication plans to ensure alignment and accountability.

Distinct roles keep the process running smoothly: Developers are responsible for delivering reliable, well-tested code and participating in planning and reviews; Product Managers own the vision, roadmap, and metrics, prioritizing work for maximum value; Project Managers coordinate timelines, risks, and documentation, facilitating stakeholder engagement and team communication. These personas clarify responsibilities and help maintain focus on both technical delivery and business outcomes.

Communication is intentional and structured, with daily or twice-weekly standups, weekly syncs, and regular stakeholder updates. The project board serves as the primary source of truth for work status, while risk registers and standardized templates for status and incident updates ensure transparency—especially when escalations are required. After each sprint or major event, the team conducts retrospectives to identify opportunities for continuous improvement and to capture actionable learnings.

Quality assurance is integrated at multiple levels. Every change undergoes automated tests and code linting in CI, with small pull requests and peer review required before merging. Developers write unit, integration, and smoke tests, while the release process mandates verified acceptance criteria, draft release notes, and a rollback plan. Manual QA, security scans, and regular demos further ensure that releases meet expectations and maintain the project’s high standards for reliability and customer value.
